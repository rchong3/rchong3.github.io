Mari Kimura is an innovative violinist and composer who is well-known for her research in computer-augmented music.

On the violin, Kimura is both a virtuosic performer and improviser. Additionally, her experimentation with violin technique led to her introducing the world to subharmonics, a technique that extends the violin's range up to a full octave below the lowest note it can naturally play. Kimura has incorporated subharmonics into her own compositions including 6 Caprices for Subharmonics (1997-1998), Gemini for solo violin (1993), and her cadenza for Jean-Claude Risset's Violin Concerto Sch√©mes (2006-2007). Since 2017, Kimura has been a Professor of Music at UC Irvine's ICIT (Integrated Composition, Improvisation, and Technology) program in the Claire Trevor School of the Arts Music Department. In September 2020, she released a her motion sensor, MUGIC, commercially, making it more accessible for musicians to interact with and control technology during performances.

Mastering the Violin

Mari Kimura was born and raised in Tokyo, Japan. She earned a Bachelor of Music from the Toho School of Music studying violin performance under Toshia Eto. On Eto's recommendation, Kimura would go to Boston University to pursue a Master in Music for violin performance under Roman Totenberg, a friend of her former professor.
